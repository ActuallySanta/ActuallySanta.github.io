Don't Lose your head!

October 2025 - October 2025

Don't Lose Your Head is a puzzle platformer made in a week for the 2025 Scream Jam. Play as a skeleton collecting as they navigate a haunted manor utilizing the fact that they can throw their head to activate conveniently placed levers and buttons to collect candy for their friends!

I personally worked as the project lead and one of the main programmers. I also had to step in and do some of the environmental art due to some restructuring during the jam itself.

Overall I learned a lot about what it takes to work with a team and how to lead one, I learned that consistent meetings and attention to the overall progress make it easy to catch issues before they snowball into something unmanageable. I also learned how to scope a project to the amount of time that we had and what to prioritize to make sure we have a complete experience before beginning to polish

Dungeon Raider

April 2025 - May 2025

Forced to fulfill a never-ending quota forced upon you by your late father's debtors (you should really have your lawyers look into that I don't think it's legal), you descend into the magical dungeon filled with foul monsters but also valuable gold.

While you're down there you may encounter a mysterious shop run by a shadowy figure that may sell you items and abilities in exchange for some of the loot that you've collected so far...

I worked as the project lead as well as the main artist for the majority of this project, along with contributing a lot of the code for the baseline systems and the shop system present in the game.

This was my first attempt at being the lead for a project, and while I'm proud of what I was able to complete I definitely know that I had room to grow after this experience. For starters, I didn't make the decision to take lead until a large portion of our time had already been used up. This left me with less time to make the project and I would've been able to make a much better project if I had been able to better utilize the time given. Second I had never lead meetings like this before so early on I struggled to focus on the important parts of the meeting that needed the most amount of attention, which primarily was delegating tasks and planning for the coming sprint.

In the end we delivered a project that I was proud of but I definitely have more to learn. I'm happy that I was able to learn about what it takes to lead a project like this and this was a strong first forey into the topic itself.
